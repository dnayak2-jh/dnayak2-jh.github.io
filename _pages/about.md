I am a Ph.D. candidate in Civil and Systems Engineering at Johns Hopkins University, advised by [Dr. Somdatta Goswami](https://engineering.jhu.edu/faculty/somdatta-goswami/). My research focuses on **AI for Science** and **Scientific Machine Learning (SciML)**, with a particular focus on developing scalable, efficient, reliable, and mesh-invariant neural surrogates to accelerate numerical simulations of time-dependent PDEs.

Prior to joining JHU, I interned with the AI research team at **HARMAN International**, developing computer vision applications for automotive infotainment and ADAS systems. I hold a master's degree in Computational and Data Sciences from the **Indian Institute of Science (IISc) Bangalore**, where I worked on dimensionality reduction methods for combustion simulations, and a bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from **NIT Tiruchirappalli**. Outside of research, I enjoy soccer, badminton, and chess. I'm also a gym enthusiast and love cooking various cuisines (though eating them remains my true expertise).
